Introduction

Dataset Description

For this analysis, I have chosen the nowshowappointments dataset. Within this dataset there is a plethora of demographic information on patients that have schedueled appointments and have either show up or not. There is a total of 110527 rows and 14 columns. The 14 columns are as follows:

PatientID, AppointmentID, Gender, ScheduledDay, AppointmentDay, Age, Neighbourhood, Scholarship, Hipertention, Diabetes, Alcoholism, Handcap, SMS-Recieved, No-show.

The significance of each column will depend on the questions being asked, but for a broad description, the first 2 columns, PatientID and AppointmentID, give idenfication numbers for the indiviual and the appointment itself, allowing for easy calculating and tracking. Columns Gender through Neighbourhood give valuable demographic information about each patient. Scholarship through Handicap provide insight into whether the patient has any pre-existing conditions. SMS-recieved gives details on whether the patient recieved a text reminding them of their appointment, and No-show states whether the patient showed up or not, "No" meaning they did show up and "Yes" meaning they did not.

Question(s) for Analysis

Q1: Does gender and age play a role in whether a patient shows up for an appointment?

Q2: Which neighbourhood is associated with the highest no-show rate?

Conclusions

Q1

Throughout this analysis, I found that while both male and female patients tend to follow the same regression in terms of missing or attending their appointments, females tend to miss and attend more appointments within the 20-65 age bracket. That being said both genders tend to taper off in terms of overall appointment number as they get older. Additional research can be done through a more statistical lense by finding how specific each age group or gender plays a role on how many appointments are missed.

Q2

I found that the majority of appointments occur in a select few neighbourhoods and the one with the highest rate of missed appointments is Jardim Camburi, with a ratio of 5.26 to 1, missed to not missed. More research into a wider range of neighbourhoods could be done to start to label which neighbourhoods can be expected to produce a no show.

Limitations

A limitation for this analysis is the lack of informational data in reference to the objective of the data. The dataset has columns listing pre-existing conditions which is good information, but irrelevant for the goal of the data. Information on the reason behind why a patient missed would have allowed for a more indepth analysis to eventually begin to predict when a person might miss an appointment at the time they schedule it.
